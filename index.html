Biography

Tao Xiang is the founder of JouleBeat and an algorithmic technology entrepreneur whose work connects computer vision, spatiotemporal intelligence, risk modeling, and autonomous systems. His focus is consistent across these fields: finding structure in complex real-world data, turning that structure into decisions, and building systems that can act on those decisions reliably.

His technical foundation was formed in remote sensing at Wuhan University and in Information Engineering at The Chinese University of Hong Kong, where he was advised by Prof. Xiaoou Tang. That training shaped a long-running interest in visual and multimodal representation, spatial and temporal uncertainty, and the path from an algorithmic result to an operational signal.

Across his ventures, Tao has applied this capability stack to commercial earth intelligence, global crop monitoring, algorithmic weather-risk pricing, and autonomous AI systems. These are not separate technical chapters so much as different expressions of the same underlying work: using algorithms to understand changing environments, price uncertainty, trigger decisions, and coordinate execution.

Today, alongside building JouleBeat, Tao contributes to the OpenClaw runtime and created the OpenClaw AI Ecosystem Sessions—a recurring technical interface connecting AI companies, hardware teams, and developers across regions. He also authored a public application playbook that turns ecosystem collaboration into a transparent, reusable community process.
