01

JouleBeat: Public Systems

Public, inspectable systems demonstrating JouleBeat’s approach to state, verification, recovery, and constrained autonomous execution.

Autonomous software evolution

Evolution Kernel ↗

An explicit observe → plan → execute → evaluate → commit runtime for auditable, reversible codebase self-improvement.

  • Git-backed ledger, commit and rollback for every accepted change
  • Allowed-path enforcement, Firejail-based filesystem isolation and execution controls, and hard cost, token, iteration, and failure limits
  • Durable execution across restarts, with roles decoupled through inspectable files
  • Public v1.0: ~1,900 lines of Python runtime, 99 acceptance and unit tests, and four adapters
Autonomous research

DeepGraph ↗

An evidence-grounded research system designed to move from a research direction to checkable conclusions and reproducible experiments.

Implemented
  • Evidence maps, experiment plans, claim grounding, and provenance tracking
  • Experiment orchestration with multi-seed result handling
  • Deterministic evidence gates that can pass or block downstream output
Design principles
  • Negative-result preservation and traceable claims
  • Significance, leakage, and compute-matched baseline checks
  • Human review at consequential decision points
These are public technical systems demonstrating JouleBeat’s runtime principles. They are presented as inspectable engineering evidence, not as production-scale benchmarks.

Background

Tao Xiang is a technology founder with a long-running focus on algorithms that must work in changing, uncertain environments—from observing the physical world, to predicting risk, to enabling autonomous systems to act reliably.

Trained in remote sensing at Wuhan University and in Information Engineering at The Chinese University of Hong Kong under Prof. Xiaoou Tang, he has built businesses and systems spanning commercial earth intelligence, commercial crop monitoring across major production regions worldwide, algorithmic weather-risk pricing, and agent infrastructure. Today he leads JouleBeat and contributes to open-source agent systems and cross-region AI ecosystem collaboration.
